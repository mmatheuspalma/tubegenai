Title: What It Means When Your Dog Barks At Night

When your dog barks at night, you probably think they're just being loud. Maybe they heard something. Maybe they're bored. But what's actually happening is far more specific than most owners realize, and it reveals something about your dog's internal state that you've likely been misreading for years.

Dogs don't bark at night for the same reason they bark during the day. Their brain operates differently after dark. Canine sensory processing shifts significantly in low-light conditions. Their hearing becomes more acute. Their territorial instincts heighten. And their threshold for what feels threatening drops considerably. That random bark at two in the morning isn't random at all.
